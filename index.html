MUSIC370 FINAL: LOVE(-ADJACENT)

By Jane Plomp

Here is a collection of songs written and produced for my music 370 class. The theme I went with was "Love(-Adjacent)". I like to write songs that I haven't heard before, and since love songs are so pervasive I tend to shy away from writing those. However, I think there are a lot of things I love and things that relate to love in different ways that can be explored, so I used love and love-adjacent subjects as an overarching theme.

Hungry Kitty

This is for my cat, Chaz, who I love. There's a small section in dutch since some of my family members occasionally speak to her in that language. I had a dutch consultant (my dad) help me with the written lyrics, but please forgive imperfections in my pronounciation.

Better For You

I've never had a messy break-up, but I love break-up/diss/revenge songs.

Mothman

I've included Mothman in this project because I think he deserves more love. Honestly though, everyone goes on about bigfoot, sasquatch, nessie, etc., but mothman is among the most charming cryptids I'm aware of.

Measure my Love

The closest thing to a true love song in this collection, though it's more about love of quantification than necessarily love for a person.

The Swarm

This song came to me in a dream. Specifically, a nightmare where I was presenting my progress to the class on this project, and when I tried to play "Mothman" this played instead. Well, not exactly this. The dream version was much gentler, but I ended up composing a creepier version. I love bees, which makes it fit with the theme of this project, but unfortunately this song presents bees as a little bit scary.

Nice Guy

This song has a lot of vulgar language. If you are a child, please skip this song. I don't love nice guys (not the type that I critique in this song, anyway) but I think their misogyny sometimes comes from their belief that they love women.




PREVIOUS PROJECTS FOR THIS CLASS

I've written various other pieces for this class, though not specifically for this project. If you're curious about other works I've made, here they are:

O-Robot

This project theme was stumbled upon a little bit by accident... I started with "Override", then happened to read about the Mars rover Opportunity dying and felt inspired to write "Opportunity". Upon reflection, I discovered I wrote two songs about robots starting with the letter "O". Two songs feels like just a coincidence, so I wanted a third to make it a proper theme. That's when "0281-Wife" popped up, and although a zero is not exactly an "O", I pronounce it as an "O" in the song.

Opportunity

The inspiration for this piece was, obviously, the Mars rover Opportunity. I'd like if there were more serenades to real life robots.

Override

I love that robot media is an opportunity to explore personhood. Although I use robots, I think there's a universal message of the sort of questioning one undergoes due to marginalization, a theme I personally relate to through womanhood. This song has a message of realizing one's own personhood and worth, despite being told otherwise.

0281-Wife

The theme of womanhood and fighting marginalization is a little more explicit in this narrative song. I love songs that tell a story, and this is a funky sci-fi sort of story with a big pro-robot message.
